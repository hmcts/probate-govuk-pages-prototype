Applying for probate

You can apply for probate yourself if you're an executor. A solicitor or another person licensed to provide probate services can also apply on your behalf.

Before you apply, you must estimate and report the estate’s value. Depending on the value, you may have to pay Inheritance Tax.

Apply online

You can use this service if you're the executor and you have:

  • the original will
  • the original death certificate or an interim death certificate from the coroner

The person who died must have lived in England or Wales most of the time.

Apply for probate
Return to an existing probate application


Apply by post

If the person who died left a will, fill in the probate application form PA1P.

If the person who died did not leave a will, fill in the probate application form PA1A.

Apply by post if you want to apply in Welsh.

Sending documents

You'll need to send some documents by post to support your application. Use a signed-for postal service.

If you apply online

You'll need to send the original will and any updates ('codicils'). You may need to send some other documents, for example the death certificate.

After you submit your application you'll be told exactly what documents to send and where to send them.

If you apply by post

You'll need to send these documents to your local probate registry along with your application form:

  • the original will and any updates
  • 2 copies of the signed will and any additions on plain A4 paper (do not remove any staples or bindings to make the copies)
  • the death certificate or an interim death certificate from the coroner

Returned documents

The death certificate will be returned to you but the original will and any updates will not be - this is because they become part of public record.

You may want to make copies of the will for your own records.

Changes or damage to the will

You must include a cover letter when you send the will if it has changed in any way or been damaged since you've had it. This includes staples or bindings being removed.

Explain in the letter what has been changed and why.

Fees

The application fee is based on the value of the estate. There's no fee if the estate is £50,000 or under.

Estate value Fee
£50,000 or under Free
Over £50,000 to £300,000 £250
Over £300,000 to £500,000 £750
Over £500,000 to £1 million £2,500
Over £1 million to £1.6 million £4,000
Over £1.6 million to £2 million £5,000
Over £2 million £6,000

To pay:

  • apply online and pay by debit or credit card
  • call the probate registry between 9:30am and 3pm and pay by debit or credit card - you’ll be given a reference number to send with your documents
  • send a cheque payable to HM Courts & Tribunals Service with your documents

Extra copies of the grant of probate cost £10 for the first copy and 50 pence for every copy after that. This means you can send them to different organisations at the same time.
